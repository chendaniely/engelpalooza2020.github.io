What is Engelpalooza?

Engelpalooza is a research showcase hosted by the Virginia Tech Department of Biochemistry. Engelpalooza offers undergraduate and graduate students involved in biochemistry and research a unique forum to showcase their research, exchange ideas, and improve their communication skills, all while networking with members of the Virginia Tech scientific community. This event also allows undergraduate seeking research opportunities to see the various ways they can get involved. This event is sponsored by Virginia Tech's Biochemistry Department, and made possible by the Howard Hughes Medical Institute Inclusive Excellence initiative.

Engelpalooza 2020 will be held as a virtual poster symposium on the communication platform, Discord.

Organizers

Engelpalooza is supported by HHMI Building Inclusive Excellence in the Sciences at Virginia Tech grant program. This program seeks to improve the learning environments for students from underrepresented regions of Virginia and all students in the Virginia Tech sciences. We work to foster inclusion and success through inclusive pedagogy addressing implicit biases and issues in STEM classrooms and creating curricular paths that are flexible and embedded wiht high impact practices.

Members of the Biochemistry HHMI Dept Cohort are: Anne Brown, Glenda Gillaspy, Kristopher Hite, Peter Kennelly, Michael Klemba, Sasha Marine, Amy Rasor, and Chevon Thorpe. We also thank and recognize all student support, including peer mentors.
